# Big Pants Apps Design Guide

## Brand Idea

Big Pants Apps is an independent app studio for small, useful apps with a little personality.

The name should feel memorable, friendly, and confident. It should not feel like a joke site. The design should make the playful name feel intentional: warm colors, clear writing, crisp app presentation, and a quietly polished layout.

Core brand phrase:

> Small apps. Big usefulness.

Supporting idea:

> We make focused mobile apps that do one job well, without clutter, fuss, or a fifty-tab settings maze.

## Personality

Big Pants Apps should feel:

- Friendly
- Practical
- Slightly playful
- Trustworthy
- Independent
- Clear

It should avoid feeling:

- Corporate
- Generic
- Gimmicky
- Childish
- Overly slick
- Like a theme, wallpaper, or font app company

The name carries the fun. The page design should carry the trust.

## Color Palette

Use the provided Dark Sunset palette as the foundation.

Primary colors:

- Dark Slate Grey: `#335C67`
- Vanilla Custard: `#FFF3B0`
- Honey Bronze: `#E09F3E`
- Brown Red: `#9E2A2B`
- Black Cherry: `#540B0E`

Recommended roles:

- `#335C67`: Primary brand color, nav, headings, links, app-card accents
- `#FFF3B0`: Warm page background, soft panels, subtle highlights
- `#E09F3E`: Primary call-to-action color, badges, active states, small graphic details
- `#9E2A2B`: Secondary emphasis, hover states, alert-style accents, limited decorative use
- `#540B0E`: Deep text color, footer background, strong contrast moments

Suggested neutrals:

- Page background: `#FFF9DD` or `#FFF7CF`
- Main text: `#2A1818`
- Muted text: `#6A5552`
- Border: `rgba(84, 11, 14, 0.14)`
- Card background: `#FFFCED`

Color usage notes:

- Keep the page warm and light. Do not make the whole site dark red or dark teal.
- Use the deep red and black cherry sparingly so the brand feels cozy, not heavy.
- Let app icons and screenshots add visual variety.
- The orange should be used as an action color, not as a full-page theme.

## Typography

The type should be round enough to match the name, but crisp enough to feel professional.

Recommended pairing:

- Headings: `Fraunces`, `Cooper Black`, `Recoleta`, or `Source Serif 4`
- Body/UI: `Inter`, `Nunito Sans`, `Avenir`, or system sans-serif

Practical web default:

```css
font-family: Inter, ui-sans-serif, system-ui, -apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, "Segoe UI", sans-serif;
```

Heading style:

- Large but not oversized
- Confident line height
- No negative letter spacing
- Prefer sentence case

Body style:

- Plain, readable, and direct
- Avoid startup jargon
- Keep app descriptions to one or two sentences

## Logo Direction

The logo can be simple and wordmark-first.

Recommended concept:

- Text mark: `Big Pants Apps`
- Optional icon: a simplified pants pocket, stitched patch, or rounded app tile with a small pocket shape
- Avoid a literal cartoon pair of pants as the main logo unless it is highly simplified

Logo tone:

- Friendly, blocky, and sturdy
- Slightly rounded edges
- Works in one color
- Must be legible at small footer/nav sizes

Potential mark ideas:

- A square app icon with a pocket stitched into it
- A folded label/tag shape with "BP"
- A rounded badge with two vertical pocket lines
- A simple monogram: `BP`

## Page Goal

The landing page should be an app catalog first. A visitor should immediately understand:

1. This is Big Pants Apps.
2. The studio makes small, useful mobile apps.
3. There are three apps available right now.
4. Each app has a clear App Store link.

Avoid a long marketing page. The apps should be visible in the first viewport or just below it.

## Recommended Page Structure

### Header

Content:

- Logo/wordmark: `Big Pants Apps`
- Nav links: `Apps`, `About`, `Support`
- Optional button: `Contact`

Design:

- Light warm background
- Dark teal logo
- Simple nav
- Sticky header is optional

### Hero

Headline:

> Small apps. Big usefulness.

Subheadline:

> Big Pants Apps makes focused mobile tools for everyday moments: tracking, training, learning, and getting things done without extra clutter.

Primary button:

> Browse Apps

Secondary link:

> Get Support

Design:

- Use the hero to establish personality, not to hide the apps.
- Add a small visual system, such as three overlapping app tiles, each using one brand color.
- Avoid a generic phone mockup unless real screenshots are available.
- Keep the hero compact enough that the app list is hinted on desktop and mobile.

### Apps Section

Section title:

> Our Apps

Section intro:

> A small collection of practical apps, each built around one clear job.

Each app card should include:

- App icon or placeholder tile
- App name
- Category
- One-sentence description
- App Store link
- Optional status badge, such as `Available on iPhone`

Cards should be compact and scannable. Do not bury the App Store link.

### About Section

Title:

> About Big Pants Apps

Copy:

> Big Pants Apps is an independent studio building simple, useful mobile apps. We like tools that are easy to understand, quick to open, and pleasant enough to keep using.

Optional second paragraph:

> The studio name is playful on purpose. The work behind it is careful: clear interfaces, focused features, and apps that respect your time.

### Support Section

Title:

> Support

Copy:

> Need help with one of our apps, have a privacy question, or want to report a bug? Send us a note and tell us which app you are using.

Links:

- Contact
- Privacy Policy
- Terms of Use

### Footer

Content:

- `Big Pants Apps`
- `Small apps. Big usefulness.`
- Links: `Apps`, `Support`, `Privacy`
- Copyright line

Footer design:

- Black Cherry background
- Vanilla Custard text
- Honey Bronze links or hover states

## App Copy

Use the following as starter copy. Replace placeholder App Store links when the final URLs are available.

### Pregnancy Tracker

Category:

> Health & Family

Short description:

> A calm, easy pregnancy tracker for following milestones, dates, and weekly progress.

Longer card copy:

> Track pregnancy progress week by week with a simple app that keeps the important details close and the noise out.

CTA:

> View on the App Store

Notes:

- Tone should be gentle, clear, and supportive.
- Avoid medical claims unless the app has medically reviewed content.
- Include a disclaimer on the app detail page or support page if needed: this app is informational and is not a substitute for medical advice.

### Blackjack Trainer

Category:

> Games & Training

Short description:

> Practice blackjack decisions and sharpen your basic strategy one hand at a time.

Longer card copy:

> Build confidence at the table with fast practice rounds, clear feedback, and a focused way to improve your blackjack strategy.

CTA:

> View on the App Store

Notes:

- Position as a trainer and learning tool.
- Avoid implying guaranteed winnings.
- If the app includes casino-style content, add age-appropriate wording where required.

### Flash Sign

Category:

> Utilities

Short description:

> Turn your phone into a bright, readable sign when you need to get a message seen.

Longer card copy:

> Display bold full-screen messages for pickups, events, rides, crowds, or any moment when a clear sign beats waving your arms.

CTA:

> View on the App Store

Notes:

- This app can carry the most playful tone.
- Consider showing example messages in screenshots: `TAXI`, `PICKUP`, `OVER HERE`, `THANK YOU`.
- Emphasize readability, speed, and usefulness.

## Visual System

Use simple, sturdy shapes:

- App tiles
- Pocket-like labels
- Small stitched-line details
- Rounded squares with 6px to 8px radius
- Thin borders in dark cherry at low opacity

Avoid:

- Oversized rounded cards
- Decorative blobs
- Purple gradients
- Abstract tech illustrations
- Overly cute pants graphics
- Heavy shadows

Card style:

- Background: `#FFFCED`
- Border: `rgba(84, 11, 14, 0.14)`
- Radius: `8px`
- Shadow: very subtle, or none
- App icon size: 56px to 72px
- CTA aligned consistently

Button style:

- Primary button background: `#E09F3E`
- Primary button text: `#2A1818`
- Primary hover: `#9E2A2B`
- Primary hover text: `#FFF3B0`
- Secondary links: `#335C67`

## App Card Layout

Recommended card fields:

```text
[Icon]
Pregnancy Tracker
Health & Family
A calm, easy pregnancy tracker for following milestones, dates, and weekly progress.
[View on the App Store]
```

For three apps:

- Desktop: 3-column grid
- Tablet: 2-column grid
- Mobile: 1-column grid

## Tone Of Voice

Write like a person who respects the visitor's time.

Good:

- "Focused mobile apps for everyday moments."
- "Open the app, do the thing, move on with your day."
- "The name is playful. The work is careful."

Avoid:

- "Revolutionary"
- "Next-generation"
- "Seamless ecosystem"
- "AI-powered" unless it is actually central to the product
- "Beautifully crafted experiences" unless the page shows the craft

## Implementation Notes

- The first screen should show the brand and at least the top of the app list.
- Use real app icons as soon as they are available.
- Use official App Store badges only if following Apple's badge guidelines. Otherwise, use simple text buttons.
- If each app has its own privacy policy, link from the app card or app detail page.
- If all apps share one privacy policy, include it clearly in the footer.
- Keep the site fast and static unless there is a strong reason to add a framework.
