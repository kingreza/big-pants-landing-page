How it works

Three quick steps

Scan or type the barcode

Use the camera scanner for packaged foods, or manually enter the barcode if the label is bent, small, or hard to read.

Match against the product database

The app looks up the barcode through our Open Food Facts-backed product database and distinguishes missing products from server errors.

Review the context

Use the score, nutrition summary, ingredients, additives, allergens, and completeness notes as a starting point for reading the label more clearly.

FAQ

Good to know

What can I use What Am I Eating for?

Use it to scan packaged food barcodes and get a faster read on the label: product score, nutrition context, ingredients, additives, allergens, and missing-data notes.

Can I scan any food product?

It is designed for packaged foods with barcodes. Fresh produce, prepared meals, restaurant food, and products without barcode records may not have enough data to show a useful result.

What does the product score mean?

The score is a quick summary based on available product data, such as nutrition values, ingredients, additives, Nutri-Score, NOVA group, and missing-data confidence. It helps you read the label; it does not make the decision for you.

Why might a product score be missing or uncertain?

Some barcode records are incomplete, outdated, or user-contributed. When important fields are missing, the app is designed to say so clearly.

Can What Am I Eating help with additives?

Yes, when additive tags are available. The app can surface conservative additive guidance, including caution labels, but those labels are informational and do not mean an ingredient is unsafe in every context.

Can I use it for allergies?

The app can show allergen tags when they are available in the product record, but it should not be your only allergy check. Always verify the package label and follow professional guidance for safety-critical food decisions.

What if a product is not found?

The app shows a product-not-found state instead of guessing. You can also enter the barcode manually if the camera cannot read the package.

Does it replace reading the nutrition label?

No. What Am I Eating is a companion to the label. Packaging should remain the source of truth, especially for allergens, ingredients, and serving details.

Is this a calorie counter or diet app?

No. The first version focuses on barcode lookup and product understanding. It is not a meal tracker, diet planner, weight-loss program, or medical recommendation tool.

Where does the food data come from?

The app is built around Open Food Facts data served through our own backend. Open Food Facts is an open, user-contributed food product database, so coverage and detail can vary by product and region.

Is What Am I Eating the same as Yuka?

No. What Am I Eating is independent. It does not use Yuka data, and its product guidance is based on open food data and our own scoring approach.

Does it save my scan history?

The first version is designed around local recent scans and cached product details so you can revisit products on your device. Accounts and cloud sync are not part of the MVP.
