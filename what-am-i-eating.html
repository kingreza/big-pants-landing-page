How it works

Three quick steps

Scan or type the barcode

Use the camera scanner for packaged foods, or manually enter the barcode if the label is bent, small, or hard to read.

Match against the product database

The app looks up the barcode through our Open Food Facts-backed product database and distinguishes missing products from server errors.

Review the context

Use the score, nutrition summary, ingredients, additives, allergens, and completeness notes as a starting point for reading the label more clearly.

FAQ

Questions people ask before scanning

What is What Am I Eating?

It is a mobile barcode food scanner for packaged foods. It helps explain available product data, including nutrition, ingredients, additives, allergens, a score, and data-completeness warnings.

Where does the product data come from?

The product is built around Open Food Facts data served through our own backend. Open Food Facts is open and user-contributed, so coverage and accuracy can vary.

Is this using Yuka data?

No. What Am I Eating is independent and uses Open Food Facts data with our own scoring approach.

Can product data be missing or inaccurate?

Yes. The app is designed to show when important fields are missing and when a score is based on limited available information.

Does it give medical or nutrition advice?

No. It is informational only. For allergies, medical conditions, pregnancy, strict diets, or other health questions, verify the package and talk with a qualified professional.

Does it show additives and allergens?

Yes, when those tags are available in the product record. Always check the package label before making safety-critical decisions.

What if a product is not found?

The app shows a product-not-found state instead of guessing. Barcode entry is also available when scanning does not work.

Is it a calorie tracker or diet planner?

No. The first version focuses on barcode lookup and product understanding, not meal tracking, diet plans, accounts, or cloud sync.
